About

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Engineering at The University of Texas at Dallas, with research interests in AI-powered video monitoring, computer vision, and healthcare analytics. My academic background spans computer science, machine learning, pattern recognition, database design, and intelligent systems, providing a strong foundation for developing advanced AI solutions.

I have spent more than three years as a Research Assistant at the Predictive Analytics & Technologies Laboratory at UT Dallas under the supervision of Dr. Mehrdad Nourani, contributing to research in healthcare AI, computer vision, and intelligent monitoring technologies. In addition, I have served as a Teaching Assistant for two courses, where I supported student learning and strengthened my ability to communicate complex technical concepts effectively.

I hold both a Master’s and Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Shahid Bahonar University in Iran. My master’s thesis focused on routing algorithms in mobile ad hoc networks, and during my undergraduate studies I ranked first in the Computer Science Department. My experience across research, teaching, and academic training has shaped my passion for building practical, scalable, and impactful AI systems for real-world applications.
